About This Commentary

A narrative and academic engagement with the whole of Dante's poem

This site presents a canto-by-canto prose commentary on all one hundred cantos of the Divina Commedia. Each entry summarizes the action of the canto and then enters into its theological, political, and literary substance — the contrapasso of Hell, the philosophy of love in Purgatory, the light-metaphysics of Paradise.

The commentary is written for a serious general reader. It assumes no knowledge of medieval Italian or Scholastic philosophy, but neither does it retreat from complexity. Dante's poem is, among other things, a sustained argument about the nature of justice, the ordering of desire, the relationship between reason and grace, and the proper ends of human civilization — and these arguments deserve to be followed wherever they lead.

Each canticle can be read in full — use the "Expand All" button — or explored one canto at a time. The three sections are designed to be read in order, as Dante wrote them: downward through Hell, upward through Purgatory, and outward into the light that defeats all language at the poem's final line: l'amor che move il sole e l'altre stelle — the love that moves the sun and all the other stars.
